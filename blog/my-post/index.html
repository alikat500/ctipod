What is Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI)?

Bridging Threat Awareness and Action in Modern Cybersecurity

In an era where cyber threats are growing in frequency, sophistication, and impact, organizations can no longer afford to be reactive. Instead, they must anticipate, understand, and prepare for attacks before they occur. This is where Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) comes in—a critical discipline that enables cybersecurity teams and executives to make informed, proactive decisions.

Understanding Cyber Threat Intelligence

Cyber Threat Intelligence is the collection, evaluation, and application of information about current and potential cyber threats. CTI is more than just data; it is analyzed information that provides context—who the adversaries are, what their tactics and goals might be, and how to defend against them.

CTI transforms scattered indicators and raw logs into actionable intelligence, helping both technical and non-technical stakeholders:

  • SOC analysts can fine-tune detection rules.

  • Threat hunters can identify patterns of compromise.

  • Executives can prioritize cybersecurity investments based on real risk.

There are generally four types of CTI:

  1. Strategic – High-level intelligence tailored for executives. It includes geopolitical or industry trends.

  2. Operational – Details on adversaries’ campaigns, tools, and behaviors.

  3. Tactical – Technical indicators (IOCs) like IPs, hashes, and domains.

  4. Technical – Raw, unprocessed data that often needs correlation or enrichment.
